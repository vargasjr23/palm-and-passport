Key Findings
The Palm & Passport landing page has strong on-page content — a keyword-optimized title ("Panama Relocation Concierge"), a well-structured heading hierarchy, and a compelling brand voice. However, it was missing every major technical SEO layer: no JSON-LD schema, no canonical tag, no Twitter cards, incomplete Open Graph tags, no sitemap.xml, and no robots.txt. All three top-ranking competitors (StartAbroad, AskPanama, RetireInPanama) have full schema suites with FAQPage, Organization, and WebPage markup, giving them featured snippet eligibility that Palm & Passport currently lacks. Word count at ~916 words is significantly below the competitor average of 1,500–2,200 words. The most impactful remaining opportunity is adding a FAQ section and visa-specific content (Friendly Nations Visa, Pensionado Visa) — these are present on every competing page and represent both content depth and structured-data ranking opportunities.

Content Gaps vs. Competitors

No visible FAQ section

All 3 competitors dedicate a full section to FAQs. AskPanama's FAQPage schema generates rich results (expandable answers) in Google search. A FAQ section with the 4 schema Q&As already added is the fastest path to featured snippet eligibility.

Add a visible FAQ section to the page (between "The Curated Journey" and the final CTA) with these 4 questions: visa types, what's included, timeline, language barrier. The FAQPage schema is already embedded — adding visible HTML makes it complete.
Critical

No specific visa program names

Every competitor explicitly mentions: Friendly Nations Visa, Pensionado Visa, Investor Visa, and Digital Nomad/Remote Worker Visa. These are high-intent search terms that people use when researching Panama relocation. The Palm & Passport page uses generic "residency" language instead.

Add visa-type names to the "Legal Strategy & Residency" card and/or a dedicated "Panama Residency Visas" subsection. At minimum: "Friendly Nations Visa", "Pensionado Visa", "Qualified Investor Visa".
High

No cost of living / pricing context

StartAbroad shows pricing ($3,950). AskPanama shows packages. Retire in Panama offers purchasable tours. Cost-related searches ("Panama relocation cost", "how much to move to Panama") have high search volume and the page misses them entirely.

Add a brief "Why Panama Makes Financial Sense" callout near the Why Panama section: typical cost of living ranges, what the service package includes, and a CTA to "Request a custom quote."
High

No geographic specificity (Panama City, Chiriquí, Boquete)

Competitors rank for location-specific searches by naming neighborhoods and regions. This is especially valuable for retirees searching "best place to retire in Panama" and families searching "best neighborhood in Panama City for expats".

Add 1–2 sentences in the "Real Estate & Lifestyle" card that mention Panama City, Chiriquí Highlands, Bocas del Toro as examples of regions Maale knows. This adds geographic LSI keywords with minimal copy.
Medium

Word count 45% below competitor average

At 916 words vs. a competitor average of ~1,700, the page is semantically thinner. Google uses topical depth as a ranking signal. More content = more LSI keyword coverage = more chances to rank for long-tail searches.

Target 1,400–1,600 words by adding: FAQ section (~200 words), visa types paragraph (~150 words), geographic section (~100 words), cost context (~100 words). This alone would close most of the gap.
Medium

Pass
